Thousands of Pis, countless cameras. The edge counts locally and sends a daily summary ("North sector: 42 capybara, 7 jaguar, 300 macaw"); a dashboard rolls it up. A jaguar, an unknown species, or a poacher fires an instant exception with proof into its folder — evidence a ranger can act on.

What devices we support — globally

TIER 1Linux edge gatewayavailable now
Raspberry Pi (3/4/5/Zero 2), Orange/Rock Pi, NVIDIA Jetson (great for on-device AI), x86 mini-PCs / industrial gateways — anything Debian/Ubuntu-family. Our installer detects OS + chip and sets it up.
TIER 2Direct HTTPS clientsworks today
Anything that can make an HTTPS call hits the ingest API directly — an ESP32 microcontroller in C, another vendor's system, a cloud function. No agent needed.
TIER 3Field-protocol adaptersroadmap
MQTT, Modbus/OPC-UA, LoRaWAN, Zigbee, BLE — spoken by the field devices, bridged at the edge by the gateway. Prebuilt read-modules + a microcontroller SDK come later.
To Chit & Bridge there is exactly one protocol: the HTTPS ingest. The edge box is the universal adapter — it speaks Modbus/MQTT/GPIO to the sensors, and one clean thing to us. That's how we stay global without boiling the ocean.
